About Brown Bares

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is native to parts of northern Eurasia and North America. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern.

There are many subspecies within the brown bear species, including the Atlas bear and Himalayan brown bear.

Features

Brown bears are not always completely brown. Some can be reddish or yellow they have very large, curved claws and huge paws. Male brown bares are often 30% larger than female brown bares. They can range from 5 feet to 9 feet from head to toe.
